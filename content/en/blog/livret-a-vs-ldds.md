Livret A and LDDS are the two most popular regulated savings passbooks in France. Their operation is very similar, but a few differences allow one or the other to be prioritised.

## Comparison table

| Criterion | Livret A | LDDS |
|---|---|---|
| Cap | 22,950 euros | 12,000 euros |
| Rate | Same regulated rate | Same regulated rate |
| Opening | From birth | From age 18 |
| French tax residence required | No | Yes, in France |
| Use of deposits | Social housing | Social economy, energy transition |

## When to prioritise the Livret A?

The Livret A is the priority to build precautionary savings up to its cap. It is accessible from birth and requires no residence condition.

<div class="callout-asavoir">
A family of 2 adults and 2 children can place up to 91,800 euros on 4 Livrets A. Useful to sanctuarise extended precautionary savings.
</div>

## When to prioritise the LDDS?

The LDDS plays the role of a second passbook once the Livret A cap is reached. It presents the same tax advantages and availability.

## Stacking strategy

The logical order for an adult individual:

1. Fill the Livret A (22,950 euros)
2. Complete with the LDDS (12,000 euros)
3. Consider other supports (LEP if eligible, life insurance, PEA)

<div class="callout-aretenir">
Livret A and LDDS are not competitors but complementary. In total, an individual can place 34,950 euros without tax or social contribution, with immediate liquidity.
</div>
