<li>Online bank: banking licence, complete products (credit, savings, stock market)</li>
<li>Neobank: fast to open, basic offers but reduced fees</li>

The French banking landscape has been largely reshaped over the past 10 years. Online banks and neobanks share a digital DNA, but their offers and guarantees differ.

## Comparison table

| Criterion | Online bank | Neobank |
|---|---|---|
| Licence | Full banking | Payment institution or bank |
| Maintenance fees | Generally free | Free for basic offer |
| Card | Free with income condition | Free with usage conditions |
| Mortgage | Yes | No (rare exceptions) |
| Regulated savings | Yes | Partially |
| Stock market and PEA | Yes | No |
| Deposit guarantee | 100,000 euros FGDR | Variable by status |

## Online bank: for whom?

Online banking suits profiles who want:

- A full main bank
- Access to all savings and credit products
- Responsive customer service without a physical branch
- Low fees but complete offer

<div class="callout-asavoir">
Opening conditions often include a minimum income (1,200 to 1,800 euros monthly) and an initial deposit. To check before applying.
</div>

## Neobank: for whom?

The neobank is rather a complementary tool:

- Secondary account for travel or daily expenses
- Fee-free payment abroad
- Quick-to-get virtual card
- Very polished mobile interface

## Points to watch

- **Legal status**: check whether it is a bank or a payment institution
- **Deposit protection**: only the FGDR fully protects French banks
- **Overdraft**: often unavailable or very restricted in neobanks
- **Cheques**: rarely accepted in neobanks

<div class="callout-aretenir">
For a main bank with credit and savings: online bank. For a mobile and international secondary account: neobank. Both are often complementary.
</div>
